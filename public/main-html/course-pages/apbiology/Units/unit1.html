  • Enzyme Structure: Proteins with active sites that bind substrates

  • Glycolysis: 2 ATP produced in cytoplasm

  • ETC: 32-34 ATP via oxidative phosphorylation

Great question! Competitive inhibitors bind to the active site, so increasing substrate concentration increases the chance substrates will "outcompete" the inhibitor. Allosteric inhibitors bind to a different site and change the enzyme's shape, so adding more substrate doesn't help.
